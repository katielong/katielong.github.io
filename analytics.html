HIV Testing Acceptance

Tawandra L. Rowell-Cunsolo, Yamnia I. Cortes, Yue Long, Erida Castro-Rivas, Jianfang Liu

We used cox regression with constant time variable to calculate risk ratios in measuring association between factors and outcome at the both bivariate and multivariate levels. Results indicated that participants who were reportedly somewhat or very religious were less likely than those who were less religious to undergo rapid HIV testing. Partcipants who were reportedly tested for HIV withiin the past year were also less likely to accept the HIV testing offer than those who had not been tested within the past year.
